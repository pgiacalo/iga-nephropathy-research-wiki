Aim 1 · Prevent / Fix the Malformed Protein

Stop Gd-IgA1 from being made — or from being made malformed — at the source (Hits 1–2).

Last updated: 2026-07-18 · Conv 1, statuses verified in Conv 2

This aim targets the upstream hits: reduce production of galactose-deficient IgA1, or correct the glycosylation defect that makes it malformed in the first place.

Reduce Gd-IgA1 production (mucosal / B-cell axis)

APRIL inhibitors First approved

APRIL (a proliferation-inducing ligand) is a key driver of pathogenic IgA1-producing plasma cells. Blocking it cuts Gd-IgA1 at the source — in the ENVISION trial, sibeprenlimab lowered circulating Gd-IgA1 by up to ~65%.

  • Sibeprenlimab (VOYXACT) (anti-APRIL mAb) — FDA accelerated approval, Nov 2025; the first drug approved specifically to lower serum Gd-IgA1. 2-year VISIONARY eGFR data reported positive Jul 2026.
  • Zigakibart / BION-1301 (anti-APRIL mAb) — Phase 3 (BEYOND).

Dual BAFF / APRIL inhibitors Now approved

  • Atacicept (TRUTAKNA) (TACI-Fc) — FDA accelerated approval, Jul 2026; reduced Gd-IgA1 ~67 pts vs placebo (ORIGIN).
  • Telitacicept (TACI-Fc) — approved in China for IgAN (2026); global Phase 3 ongoing for US/EU/Japan.
  • Povetacicept (TACI-Fc) — Phase 3 met endpoint (RAINIER); US PDUFA target Nov 30, 2026.

Plasma / B-cell depletion Phase 3

  • Felzartamab (anti-CD38 mAb) — depletes the Gd-IgA1-producing plasma cells directly; Phase 3 (PREVAIL).

Targeted gut mucosal immunity FDA-approved

  • Nefecon / Tarpeyo (targeted-release budesonide) — delivers corticosteroid to the distal ileum / Peyer's patches; the first therapy approved specifically to reduce the disease-driving mucosal immune response, not just symptoms.

Fix the glycosylation defect (the most direct "fix the protein" angle)

Most novel / under-drugged node. C1GALT1 and its chaperone Cosmc are the enzymes that galactosylate the IgA1 hinge. Restoring or modulating their activity would address the root cause rather than the downstream immunology. Today this is largely a research target — no approved drug acts here. Full deep-dive: Fix the Gd-IgA1 Glycosylation Defect.
Important caveat: a B-cell-specific c1galt1 knockout mouse over-produces Gd-IgA1 but does not develop glomerular IgA1 deposition — so Gd-IgA1 is necessary but not sufficient. Fixing the glycan alone may not prevent disease without also addressing the autoantibody / immune-complex steps. This is partly why the field has favored reducing Gd-IgA1 quantity (APRIL/BAFF, Nefecon) over correcting its quality.

Open questions for this aim

  • Is the C1GALT1/Cosmc defect genetic, epigenetic, or micro-environmentally driven (and therefore correctable)?
  • Could a protein/biologic approach clear existing Gd-IgA1 or its immune complexes rather than only reducing new production?
  • How durable is APRIL blockade — does Gd-IgA1 rebound on withdrawal?
